About Me

Welcome to my website! I am a passionate Master's student at the University of Pennsylvania, specializing in Robotics. Currently, I am working as a Research Assistant at the Figueroa Robotics Lab, affiliated with the GRASP Lab at UPenn. I hold a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Mumbai University.

My primary areas of interest within robotics are perception, robot control, and motion planning. These subjects have captivated my curiosity, and I am eager to forge a career dedicated to exploring and advancing these fields. I am driven by a relentless passion for pushing the boundaries of technology and contributing to the ever-evolving realm of robotics.

During my leisure time, I find solace in listening to music, and I enjoy engaging in sports such as cricket and badminton.

Please don't hesitate to connect with me; I'm always open to new opportunities and conversations. Thank you for visiting my website, and I look forward to sharing and exploring the exciting world of robotics together!

Projects

Control of CrazyFlie quadrotor

Implemented geometric nonlinear controller for stable control of CrazyFlie quadrotor under Dr. M. Ani Hsieh's mentorship. Developed an obstacle free path using A* algorithm and implemented minimum snap trajectory on it. Incorporated State Estimation through Visual Inertial Odometry (VIO) and Error State Kalman Filter (ESKF).

NeRF: Neural Radiance Field in 3D Vision

Implemented Neural Radiance Fields to render a 3D model of a lego model and successfully obtained a PSNR of 25.17 under the guidance of Prof. Kostas Daniilidis.
